About EVOC
Building Livelihoods in Kibera

Empowering Voice of the Community (EVOC) is a community‑based organization that seeks to empower marginalized youth through programs that aim to impart skills and knowledge necessary for employment.

Our professional and experienced team is committed to ensuring that our projects focus on achieving the vision of EVOC by equipping the marginalized youth and women in Kibera slums with skills and knowledge that will enhance their capacity to engage in meaningful socio‑economic activities.

Our Story: EVOC was established in 2018. The initiative originated at the target communities during field activities, where its founders observed the need for interventions that will assist the marginalized youth and women achieve self‑sustainability, starting with Kibera slums. After problem identification and prioritization, programs were developed to support these youth, structured based on the priority of needs and demand from the community.

Our Vision

To create a conducive environment for the marginalized youth and women in Nairobi slums to be self‑sustainable, through socio‑economic empowerment.

Our Commitment

To ensuring the marginalized youth and women embrace the strategic relevance of education as a medium for poverty reduction, social equity and development out of ignorance, has made us the partner of choice. We are committed to empower the marginalized youth and women through non‑formal education, entrepreneurship, skills training and support for them to achieve self‑sustainability.

Our Programs

Holistic interventions that tackle poverty, illiteracy, unemployment, and health challenges in Kibera slums.

Basic Education & Adult Literacy

We engage illiterate youth and women in literacy classes to impart the knowledge necessary to improve their living standards and reinforce the ideal that education is the ultimate weapon in poverty reduction and creating parity between marginalized youth and the rest of society.

Impact: 86 learners have graduated, with many now able to read, write, and manage small businesses.

TIVET & Entrepreneurship

We help bridge the gap between lack of formal education and entry into the labour market by equipping youth and women with the skills necessary for competitive participation. Courses include tailoring, carpentry, IT, and business management.

Impact: 210 trainees have completed courses; over 60% have secured employment or started their own ventures.

Sexual Reproductive Health

The SRH program aims to reduce risky behaviour among youth and women, their social networks, and the surrounding community through awareness and education, thus contributing to decreasing morbidity and mortality arising from HIV and sexual reproductive health effects.

Impact: Over 500 youth and women reached through workshops, with significant increases in knowledge and safer practices.

Sports & Culture

EVOC purposes to change the social behaviours of youth positively and nurture their talents through sporting activities as a way of reducing crime, drug and substance abuse in Kibera slums. We organize leagues, tournaments, and cultural events that promote teamwork and discipline.

Impact: 12 community teams formed; reported reduction in youth crime in participating areas.

Our Philosophy & Core Values

We believe that every marginalized youth and woman has the potential to thrive. Our work is guided by a deep commitment to accountability, transparency, and lasting change.

Accountability

We take full responsibility for our actions, resources, and outcomes, ensuring every donation and effort directly benefits the community.

Transparency

Open communication, clear reporting, and honest partnerships with donors, volunteers, and the communities we serve.

Commitment

Dedicated to long‑term empowerment, not short‑term fixes. We stay until self‑sustainability is achieved.

Competency

Our team combines expertise, local knowledge, and passion to deliver high‑impact, sustainable programs.
